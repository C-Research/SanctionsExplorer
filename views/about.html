About ArcherSDN

ArcherSDN reimagines the OFAC SDN List website, a list of individuals, entities, and vehicles that are sanctioned by the U.S. Treasury. This list is difficult to search and provides very little functionality -- yet it plays a crucial role in academic, compliance, and due diligence investigations. We've redesigned the experience of searching the SDN list, providing access to all of the same data with a vastly improved interface and additional features to streamline the investigative process.

ArcherSDN was created by Archer (a Berkeley-based nonprofit using tech to improve human rights and human security) in partnership with C4ADS (a nonprofit dedicated to providing data-driven, evidence-based research on global conflict and transnational security issues).

Features

More Robust Search

Want to find all businesses related to North Korea? What about individuals whose Chinese names can be transliterated to English in multiple ways?

With fuzzy/inexact matching, items that might have previously gone unfound will no longer slip through the cracks. Our search is forgiving of typos and alternate spellings or transliterations.

We also provide a number of additional filters, especially in regards to location (nationality, citizenship, place of birth, etc).

Press release integration

The Treasury releases additional context and justification behind sanctions via press releases that accompany updates to the SDN list. These are indexed & searchable via the ArcherSDN platform. Using the press release archive search, searches can also be conducted for entities that were sanctioned at one point but have since been removed from the SDN list (since their identifying information will have been published in a press release).

Additionally, we've algorithmically matched press releases with the entities that they discuss; ArcherSDN will attempt to display (in the regular search results) all press releases that an entity appeared in!

Additional Data

There is official Treasury data that is not available through the OFAC website, includes the reliability of information (e.g. confirmed vs reported) as well as sanction dates for most entities.

Additionally, OFAC has began included some non-Latin aliases (mostly in Cyrillic, Chinese, Japanese, and Arabic). Unfortunately, these are neither searachable nor surfaced in the results on the OFAC website (but are freely available on ArcherSDN). Note that currently a small number of entities have non-Latin names.

Printable reports

Regular search queries can be printed, allowing for immediate printable reports. Expand the entities of interest and their details will be included in the printout!

And more!

There are plenty of other ways that ArcherSDN stands out from the Treasury's website, including an interface that's modern, updated, and requires fewer clicks to navigate. If you'd like to see a feature added or report a bug, please fill out our feedback form! We continue striving for improvement and prioritize user feedback and community input when considering how to prioritize our efforts.
